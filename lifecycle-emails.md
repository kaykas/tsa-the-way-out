**Preview text:** A real path out of crisis

**CTA button text:** See How The Way Out Works

**CTA URL:** https://sanfrancisco.salvationarmy.org/san_francisco/the-way-out---get-involved

**Body:**

You signed up because you want to know what actually works in San Francisco.

The Way Out is The Salvation Army's recovery system of care for people facing addiction and homelessness. It is not a shelter bed and a hope for the best. It is a full path: treatment on demand, recovery housing, workforce development, and long-term support.

That matters because most people in crisis do not need one more disconnected service. They need a system that helps them get out and stay out.

One participant put it better than we can:

> “I came to Harbor Light to put back together the shattered pieces of my life. I'm grateful for this opportunity to start over and heal the broken parts of me — mind, body, and soul. It's helped me save my life in more ways than one. I've reunited with my children, my mother, and my brother, and I've found myself and my voice again through this program. Life isn't perfect, but since coming to Harbor Light, I can smile, laugh, and love myself again.”

That's what The Way Out is built to do.

Over the next few weeks, we'll show you how the model works, why it matters, and what real recovery looks like in San Francisco.

## Email 2 — Day 3

**Subject line:** San Francisco's crisis is bigger than tents

**Preview text:** The numbers behind the need

**CTA button text:** Learn What Makes TWO Different

**CTA URL:** https://sanfrancisco.salvationarmy.org/san_francisco/the-way-out---get-involved

**Body:**

San Francisco's addiction and homelessness crisis is easy to see.

What is harder to see is how many systems fail people because they are fragmented. A shelter here. Detox there. A referral somewhere else. No continuity. No path forward.

The Way Out is different because it is designed as one connected recovery system.

Here is what that system produced this year:

- 1,060 people served
- 69,546 nights of reduced homelessness
- Less than 1% drug test positivity rate across 1,638 tests
- 5,330 workforce development hours provided
- $473,000 in savings accumulated by recovery housing participants
- 77% success rate for people who completed the program and achieved sobriety, employment, and stable housing

Those numbers matter because they point to something deeper: recovery works better when people can move from detox to treatment to housing to employment without falling through the cracks.

The Way Out was built to close those gaps.

Jose Figueroa entered The Way Out with nothing.

Today, he is an Executive Sous Chef at Cavana.

That change did not happen because of one lucky break. It happened because recovery was matched with structure, housing, accountability, training, and the chance to build a real future.

That is the part many people miss. Recovery is not just about stopping destructive behavior. It is about building a life strong enough to support what comes next.

The Way Out does that through a full system of care, including recovery housing and the Culinary Training Academy, where participants gain professional skills they can carry into stable employment.

Jose's story is not a slogan. It is proof that when people get the right support at the right time, outcomes change.

If you've been looking for a practical, local way to make a difference, this is one.
