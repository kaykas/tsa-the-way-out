## What The Way Out Is

**Section Header:** A full system of care. Not a shelter. Not a hand-out.

**Body:**
The Way Out is The Salvation Army's recovery-focused initiative for San Francisco's homeless and addicted population. We don't offer a bed and a wish. We offer a proven path — from detox to employment, from crisis to self-sufficiency.

Our Recovery System of Care provides everything a person needs to get out and stay out:

- **Treatment on Demand** — real-time access to detox and stabilization, no waiting list
- **Two to four years of recovery housing** — paired with workforce development
- **Culinary Training Academy** — professional credentials, job placement, a career
- **Alumni Support Network** — because recovery doesn't end at program exit

80% of our participants were homeless the day they walked in.

## The System of Care (visual flow — numbered steps)

**Section Header:** From the streets to self-sufficiency. Step by step.

1. **Detox & Stabilization** — Harbor Light Wellness Center. Treatment on demand. No waiting.
2. **Drug-Free Shelter** — Hope House. Recovery-focused housing while treatment begins.
3. **Residential Drug Treatment** — Harbor Light Residential Program (6 months). Evidence-based, whole-person care.
4. **Women's Recovery** — Pinehurst ARC. Faith-based, six-month program.
5. **Recovery Housing** — Joseph McFee Center (2 years). Onsite services, case management, workforce development.
6. **Extended Housing** — Railton Place. Individual apartments for employed graduates.
7. **Independence** — Alumni Support Network. Permanent housing placement. Career guidance. Community.

*Culinary Training Academy runs in parallel at multiple stages.*
