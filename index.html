Treatment on Demand

The Way Out begins with one principle: when someone is ready to get help, they get help that day. No waitlist. No prerequisites. Treatment on Demand means real-time access to detox and stabilization — because the window of willingness is narrow, and we don't waste it.

San Francisco has thousands of people living in crisis. The Way Out is the entry point for those who want out.

"I'd asked for help before and been told to come back in two weeks. Harbor Light took me the same day. That was the difference."

— Way Out participant, 3 years in recovery FPO

Detox & Drug Free Shelter

Before recovery can begin, the body has to be safe. Our detox and drug-free shelter facilities — Harbor Light Wellness Center, Hope House, and Eleanor Fagan Stabilization Center — provide medically supported detox and a stable place to land.

This is where crisis becomes possibility. Staff meet participants where they are, without judgment, and stabilize them for the next step.

"I came in shaking. They didn't look at me like I was broken. They just helped me get through the first night, and then the next one."

— Harbor Light Wellness Center participant FPO

Drug Treatment & Recovery

Residential treatment at Harbor Light and Pinehurst Women's Recovery provides structured, long-term recovery programming — therapy, peer support, skill-building, and accountability. This is the core of The Way Out: a proven model that addresses addiction as a health condition, not a character flaw.

Pinehurst serves women specifically, with programming designed around the distinct challenges women face in recovery, including trauma and family reunification.

"Pinehurst gave me time to actually heal — not just stop using, but understand why I started. I've been clean for two years. My daughter calls me every Sunday."

— Pinehurst Women's Recovery graduate FPO

Recovery Housing & Workforce Development

Recovery without stability doesn't last. Joseph McFee Center and Marina Recovery Housing provide transitional housing — a roof, a structure, and a community — while participants build the skills they need to stay housed permanently.

The Culinary Training Academy is the workforce development engine: a real culinary program with industry credentials, externship placements, and graduates working in San Francisco restaurants.

"I got my ServSafe certification at the Culinary Academy. I'm now a line cook at a restaurant in the Mission. I have a bank account. I have a routine. I have a life."

— Culinary Training Academy graduate FPO
Support The Way Out
Independent Living

For participants who need more support before full independence, Railton Place and James Baldwin Place offer permanent supportive housing — a bridge between recovery housing and self-sufficiency, with ongoing case management and community.

This step is optional. Many participants transition directly to independent living. For those who need more time, it's here.

"Railton gave me two more years to get solid. I wasn't rushed. When I finally moved into my own place, I was ready. I haven't looked back."

— Railton Place resident, now independently housed FPO

Independence & Alumni Support

Recovery doesn't end at program exit. The Alumni Support Network keeps graduates connected — peer mentorship, community events, access to resources when life gets hard. This is what makes 77% stick.

Graduates also become the most powerful voices in the room: living proof that The Way Out works, and advocates for the people still in it.

"I went back to volunteer at Harbor Light last year. Seeing where I started and knowing where I am now — that's the whole story. I wanted someone else to see it too."

— Alumni Support Network member, 4 years independent FPO

Why The Salvation Army

The Salvation Army has served San Francisco's poor and unhoused since 1883. We're one of the city's largest nonprofit landowners. We don't just run programs — we own the infrastructure.

The Way Out isn't a pilot. It's a scaled system with a track record, a recovery model, and real results donors can measure.

A full system of care. Not a shelter. Not a hand-out.

The Way Out is The Salvation Army's recovery-focused initiative for San Francisco's homeless and addicted population. We don't offer a bed and a wish. We offer a proven path — from detox to employment, from crisis to self-sufficiency.

  • Treatment on Demand with real-time access to detox and stabilization
  • Two to four years of recovery housing paired with workforce development
  • Culinary Training Academy with credentials, placement, and a career path
  • Alumni Support Network because recovery doesn't end at program exit
