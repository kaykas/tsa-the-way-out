THE WAY OUT

The Salvation Army San Francisco

Jose Figueroa entered The Way Out with nothing.

Today, he is an Executive Sous Chef at Cavana.

That change did not happen because of one lucky break. It happened because recovery was matched with structure, housing, accountability, training, and the chance to build a real future.

That is the part many people miss. Recovery is not just about stopping destructive behavior. It is about building a life strong enough to support what comes next.

The Way Out does that through a full system of care, including recovery housing and the Culinary Training Academy, where participants gain professional skills they can carry into stable employment.

Jose's story is not a slogan. It is proof that when people get the right support at the right time, outcomes change.

If you've been looking for a practical, local way to make a difference, this is one.
