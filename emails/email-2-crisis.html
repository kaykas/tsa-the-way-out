THE WAY OUT

The Salvation Army San Francisco

San Francisco's addiction and homelessness crisis is easy to see.

What is harder to see is how many systems fail people because they are fragmented. A shelter here. Detox there. A referral somewhere else. No continuity. No path forward.

The Way Out is different because it is designed as one connected recovery system.

Here is what that system produced this year:

1,060 people served

69,546 nights of reduced homelessness

Less than 1% drug test positivity rate across 1,638 tests

5,330 workforce development hours provided

$473,000 in savings accumulated by recovery housing participants

77% success rate for people who completed the program

Those numbers matter because they point to something deeper: recovery works better when people can move from detox to treatment to housing to employment without falling through the cracks.

The Way Out was built to close those gaps.
