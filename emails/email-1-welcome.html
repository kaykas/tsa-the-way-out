THE WAY OUT

The Salvation Army San Francisco

You signed up because you want to know what actually works in San Francisco.

The Way Out — California's first Recovery System of Care — is The Salvation Army's recovery system of care for people facing addiction and homelessness. It is not a shelter bed and a hope for the best. It is a full path: treatment on demand, recovery housing, workforce development, and long-term support.

That matters because most people in crisis do not need one more disconnected service. They need a system that helps them get out and stay out.

One participant put it better than we can:

"I came to Harbor Light to put back together the shattered pieces of my life. I'm grateful for this opportunity to start over and heal the broken parts of me — mind, body, and soul. It's helped me save my life in more ways than one. I've reunited with my children, my mother, and my brother, and I've found myself and my voice again through this program. Life isn't perfect, but since coming to Harbor Light, I can smile, laugh, and love myself again."

That's what The Way Out is built to do.

Over the next few weeks, we'll show you how the model works, why it matters, and what real recovery looks like in San Francisco.
