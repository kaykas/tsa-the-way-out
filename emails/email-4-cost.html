The Salvation Army San Francisco

What does it take to help someone move from a tent to an apartment, from addiction to employment, from daily crisis to stability?

For The Way Out, the answer is about $31,000 per person per year.

That number covers what real recovery requires: treatment on demand, safe housing, case management, workforce development, and support that continues long enough to matter.

It is not cheap.

It is also far less costly than the revolving door of emergency rooms, jails, street homelessness, and repeated crisis response.

The question is not whether San Francisco is already paying for this crisis. It is.

The question is whether we will fund what actually changes the outcome.

If you believe people deserve a real way out, this is the moment to act.
